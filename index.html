Lipid-Based Delivery Technologies

A comparison of the main lipid-based systems used to improve the absorption, stability, and effectiveness of bioactive compounds.

System Key Strengths Limitations Best Suited For Maturity in Supplements
Liposomes Good absorption, versatile (water & fat-soluble compounds), biocompatible Moderate stability, potential leakage, variable quality in commercial products Vitamins, glutathione, general polyphenols, curcumin High — widely available and commonly used in consumer supplements
Phytosomes Strong molecular bond with plant compounds, clinically studied, good stability Mainly effective with specific polyphenols; less versatile for other molecules Curcumin, resveratrol, silymarin, green tea catechins High — well-established with many clinically backed branded extracts (e.g. Meriva, Siliphos)
NLCs
(Nanostructured Lipid Carriers)		 High stability, good loading capacity, controlled release, strong bioavailability gains Slightly more complex to manufacture than basic liposomes Lipophilic compounds (curcumin, resveratrol, sulforaphane, fisetin) Medium-High — growing rapidly in premium supplements
Modern LNPs
(Ionizable Lipid Nanoparticles)		 Excellent endosomal escape, very high efficiency for nucleic acids More complex and expensive, higher potential immune activation, often over-engineered for supplements mRNA/siRNA delivery, specialized pharmaceutical applications Low in supplements — primarily used in pharmaceuticals (e.g. mRNA vaccines)
TNiC APPROACH

For most longevity and cellular resilience compounds, Phytosomes and NLCs currently offer the best balance of performance, stability, and real-world applicability. Liposomes remain a solid and accessible option, while modern ionizable LNPs are generally more relevant to pharmaceutical nucleic acid delivery than daily supplement use.
