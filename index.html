Oxidative Stress & Mitochondrial Decline

Mitochondria produce most cellular energy but also generate reactive oxygen species. Cumulative damage impairs energy production and accelerates biological aging.

Inflammaging & Cellular Senescence

Senescent cells accumulate with age and secrete pro-inflammatory signals. This chronic low-grade inflammation drives tissue dysfunction.

Glutathione & NRF2 Defense Collapse

Glutathione levels decline significantly with age. NRF2 — the master regulator of antioxidant and detoxification genes — becomes less responsive.

THE MASTER REGULATOR

NRF2: Your cell’s built-in defense system.

NRF2 is a transcription factor that activates over 200 genes involved in antioxidant production, detoxification, mitochondrial protection, and inflammation control.

Why NRF2 Matters

As we age, NRF2 activity declines. This reduces the cell’s ability to neutralize oxidative stress, clear damaged proteins, and maintain mitochondrial function.

Key Functions NRF2 Controls
  • • Glutathione synthesis and recycling
  • • Phase II detoxification enzymes
  • • Mitochondrial biogenesis and quality control
  • • Anti-inflammatory and cytoprotective genes
THERAPEUTIC IMPLICATION

Pharmacological and nutritional activation of NRF2 is one of the most promising strategies for restoring cellular resilience. Compounds like sulforaphane and targeted precursors can meaningfully upregulate NRF2 activity even as natural function declines with age.

Lipid-Based Delivery Technologies

A comparison of the main lipid-based systems used to improve the absorption, stability, and effectiveness of bioactive compounds.

System Key Strengths Limitations Best Suited For Maturity in Supplements
Liposomes Good absorption, versatile, biocompatible Moderate stability, variable quality Vitamins, glutathione, general polyphenols, curcumin High — widely available and commonly used in consumer supplements
Phytosomes Strong molecular bond with plant compounds, clinically studied, good stability Mainly effective with specific polyphenols; less versatile for other molecules Curcumin, resveratrol, silymarin, green tea catechins High — well-established with many clinically backed branded extracts (e.g. Meriva, Siliphos)
NLCs
(Nanostructured Lipid Carriers)		 High stability, good loading capacity, controlled release, strong bioavailability gains Slightly more complex to manufacture than basic liposomes Lipophilic compounds (curcumin, resveratrol, sulforaphane, fisetin) Medium-High — growing rapidly in premium supplements
Modern LNPs
(Ionizable Lipid Nanoparticles)		 Excellent endosomal escape, very high efficiency for nucleic acids More complex and expensive, higher potential immune activation, often over-engineered for supplements mRNA/siRNA delivery, specialized pharmaceutical applications Low in supplements — primarily used in pharmaceuticals (e.g. mRNA vaccines)
