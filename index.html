THE MASTER REGULATOR

NRF2: Your cell’s built-in defense system.

NRF2 is a transcription factor that activates over 200 genes involved in antioxidant production, detoxification, mitochondrial protection, and inflammation control.

Why NRF2 Matters

As we age, NRF2 activity declines. This reduces the cell’s ability to neutralize oxidative stress, clear damaged proteins, and maintain mitochondrial function.

Key Functions NRF2 Controls
  • • Glutathione synthesis and recycling
  • • Phase II detoxification enzymes
  • • Mitochondrial biogenesis and quality control
  • • Anti-inflammatory and cytoprotective genes
THERAPEUTIC IMPLICATION

Pharmacological and nutritional activation of NRF2 is one of the most promising strategies for restoring cellular resilience. Compounds like sulforaphane and targeted precursors can meaningfully upregulate NRF2 activity even as natural function declines with age.

COMMON QUESTIONS

FAQ

Is it safe to combine these compounds?
These compounds have good individual safety profiles in published literature. Individual responses vary and interactions with medications are possible. Always consult a qualified healthcare provider before starting any supplementation protocol, especially if you take prescription medications or have existing health conditions.
What is NRF2 and why does it matter?
NRF2 is a transcription factor that activates your cells' built-in antioxidant and detoxification systems. When triggered, it upregulates 200+ defense genes — including those producing glutathione, catalase, and superoxide dismutase. Declining NRF2 activity with age directly correlates with increased oxidative stress and inflammaging, two core drivers of the hallmarks of aging.
Which product should I start with?
For most people, GlyNAC is the strongest entry point — it directly rebuilds glutathione, the body's master antioxidant. If NRF2 activation is your focus, Sulforaphane is the gold-standard activator with the most robust research. NMN is the right choice if NAD+ decline and mitochondrial energy are your primary concerns.
How long before I notice results?
Longevity compounds work on cellular timescales — weeks to months, not days. Mitochondrial-targeted compounds (NMN, GlyNAC) may produce noticeable energy and recovery improvements sooner. The most meaningful signals appear through lab testing: tracking NAD+, glutathione, C-reactive protein, and biological age scores over 3–6 month intervals.
