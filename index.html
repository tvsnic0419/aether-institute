THE CONTEXT

Why This Matters Now

The United States is experiencing unprecedented demographic aging. By 2040, more than one in five Americans will be over 65. This shift creates urgent pressure to understand the cellular mechanisms driving age-related decline.
THE FOUNDATION

Cellular Defense Systems

Three interconnected biological systems form the core of cellular resilience.

SYSTEM 01

Oxidative Stress Response

The network of enzymatic and non-enzymatic systems that neutralize reactive oxygen species and maintain cellular redox homeostasis.

SYSTEM 02

Inflammatory Resolution

The active processes that both initiate protective inflammation and resolve it to prevent chronic damage and support tissue repair.

SYSTEM 03

Mitochondrial Quality Control

The dynamic systems of biogenesis, fission/fusion, and mitophagy that maintain mitochondrial health and energy production capacity.

PRIMARY REGULATOR

NRF2: The Master Switch of Cellular Defense

NRF2 (Nuclear factor erythroid 2-related factor 2) is the central transcription factor that orchestrates the cellular response to oxidative and electrophilic stress.

When activated, NRF2 translocates to the nucleus and upregulates hundreds of protective genes involved in antioxidant defense, detoxification, and mitochondrial support.

KEY OUTCOMES OF NRF2 ACTIVATION
  • ↑ Antioxidant enzyme production
  • ↑ Glutathione synthesis
  • ↑ Mitochondrial biogenesis
  • ↓ Chronic inflammation signaling
  • ↑ Cellular stress resistance
